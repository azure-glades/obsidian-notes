Microeconomy -> how small units make choices (people, homes and businesses). How they are affected by market changes.
- Focuses on individual income, prices, output, consumption
- How individuals try to maximize their own welfare
- How changes in the market affect the choices of units
- Deals with supply-demand

Macroeconomy -> how many choices by small units aggregate and behaves as a whole. How this affects the market as a whole.
- Focuses on national income, inflation, trade, resources
- How resources are distributed and used across the nation
- How aggregate decisions by units affect the market as a whole (emergent behaviour)
- Deals with aggregate supply-demand, trade etc.

Difference between micro and macro economics

| Feature  | Microeconomics                                                                 | Macroeconomics                                                                       |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Define   | Study of individual economic units                                             | Study of the economy as a whole and the aggregate behaviour of units                 |
| Study    | People, Homes, Businesses and workers                                          | Global economy, economic zones, different regions and sectors of economy             |
| Factors  | Supply-demand, Prices, Utility of consumers, Profit of businesses              | GDP, National income, Unemployment, Inflation, Trade, Economic growth                |
| Aim      | What to produce? For whom to produce? Why to produce?                          | Achieve full employment, price stability and growth of economy                       |
| Approach | Bottom-up : Understand the decisions of units to see how it affects the market | Top-down : Understand trends and patterns in economy to see how market affects units |
| Tools    | Supply-demand curves, Price history, Market price, Profit-cost analysis        | National income index, Inflation rate, Growth index, Unemployment %                  |
| Terms    | Prices, Taxes, Minimum wages, Working hours                                    | Recession, Trade, Tariffs, Interest rates, Employment                                |
